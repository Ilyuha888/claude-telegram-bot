# Solutions for: Choose a PKM methodology to give the vault operational semantics

Problem: prob-20260417-001

## Variants (4)

### V-A. Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid

Statement-titled atomic evergreen notes linked by wikilinks + MOCs as navigational hubs (agent entry points) + GTD inbox-processing for Telegram captures. Four-state lifecycle: inbox→draft→evergreen→archived. Operations (projects, people, tasks) handled as typed notes (type: project/person) with checklist items inside project notes rather than a separate task system. Agent maintains topic-index.md and person-index.md in Agent vault; proposes links and MOC updates; flags stale inbox items weekly.

**Strengths:**
- Highest thinking-partner yield in literature
- MOCs are the best-known structure for agent navigation
- Statement titles dramatically improve agent keyword retrieval
- 4-state lifecycle maps directly to capture→refine pipeline
- Compatible with later adding embeddings or LLM-wiki compilation in Agent vault

**Novelty:** Unifies all four jobs under one framework by treating MOCs as both human navigation and the agent's structured entry point — no separate system for operations vs. knowledge

**Weakest link:** MOC maintenance — if MOCs fall stale, agent retrieval degrades silently and the navigational advantage collapses

**Risks:**
- MOC creation discipline required — easy to defer
- Operational notes (tasks, projects) feel less natural than a dedicated GTD app
- Requires consistent statement-title discipline from human

**Stepping stone:** yes — opens future possibilities

**Stepping-stone basis:** Frontmatter schema, lifecycle states, and dual-vault split are fully compatible with later adding GraphRAG, embeddings, or Karpathy-style LLM-wiki compilation in Agent_Obsidian_Vault

**Rollback:** All notes are plain markdown — migration to another methodology is mechanical. MOCs can be repurposed as simple hub notes.

### V-B. Karpathy LLM-Wiki Pattern

Human owns raw/ folder (captures, voice memos, fragments — never edited by agent). Agent compiles and maintains a cross-linked wiki in Agent_Obsidian_Vault/wiki/. Agent detects contradictions, creates cross-references, and maintains the compiled knowledge base. Human rarely edits compiled pages. Capture pipeline: Telegram → raw/ → agent compile → wiki page with ~8-15 wikilinks per page.

**Strengths:**
- Lowest human authoring burden
- Agent compilation produces dense cross-referencing automatically
- Scales well to high capture volume
- raw/ pattern is durable regardless of methodology changes

**Novelty:** Inverts the authorship model — the agent is the primary author of structured knowledge; the human is the primary author only of raw inputs

**Weakest link:** Semantic drift — iterative LLM rewriting smooths nuance and homogenizes voice (Nature 2024 finding); defeats the generation-effect cognitive benefit of Job 1 (thinking partner)

**Risks:**
- LLM semantic drift over time
- Human loses direct authorship of their own thinking
- Generation effect literature suggests less actual learning/insight development
- Compiled wiki pages may not reflect nuanced personal views

**Rollback:** raw/ captures are preserved and can be re-processed under a different methodology. Compiled wiki pages can be archived.

### V-C. PARA + Zettelkasten Split (IPARAG)

Explicit dual-mode structure: PARA (Projects/Areas/Resources/Archives) for operational notes (Job 2) + separate Zettelkasten 'Galaxy' zone for idea development (Job 1). The two zones use different conventions — operational notes are date-prefixed and action-oriented; Zettelkasten notes are atomic, statement-titled, and uniquely ID'd. Agent serves both zones with different navigation strategies.

**Strengths:**
- Explicit operational structure for Job 2
- Classic Zettelkasten for Job 1 is well-understood
- Clear zone boundaries reduce agent write-permission ambiguity

**Novelty:** Separates operational and conceptual knowledge into structurally different zones rather than unifying them under one methodology

**Weakest link:** PARA boundary ambiguity — the Resource vs. Area vs. Project distinction causes constant taxonomy paralysis; documented 3-year collapse rate in long-term practitioners

**Risks:**
- PARA's documented 3-year collapse from reorganization churn
- Two mental models in one vault creates cognitive overhead
- Agent must support two navigation strategies simultaneously
- Resource vs Area ambiguity is the #1 reported friction point in PARA communities

**Rollback:** Zettelkasten zone can be preserved; PARA zone can be reorganized or discarded. Migration cost is medium — depends on how much PARA structure was built.

### V-D. Minimal Substrate + Deferred Methodology

Add only: (1) frontmatter schema (type, status, created, source — four fields), (2) 4-state lifecycle (inbox/draft/evergreen/archived), (3) controlled tag vocabulary (15 seed tags). No MOCs, no statement titles, no formal capture pipeline yet. Agent files captures into inbox/, flags stale items, and proposes links opportunistically. Run for 3 months, observe actual usage patterns, then choose a methodology from evidence.

**Strengths:**
- No upfront taxonomy debt
- Frontmatter + lifecycle is compatible with any future methodology (V-A, V-B, or V-C)
- Lowest migration cost from current structure
- Strongest stepping stone to any future choice

**Novelty:** Defers the methodology commitment to after observed usage — treats the methodology choice itself as a hypothesis to be tested rather than a design decision

**Weakest link:** Emergence risk — 'structure will emerge from use' often doesn't happen; without scaffolding the vault stays a filing cabinet and the 3-month decision point gets deferred indefinitely

**Risks:**
- Collector's fallacy risk is high without processing discipline
- Linking stays ad-hoc — thinking-partner yield delayed 3+ months
- High risk of 'emergence' deferral beyond 3 months
- Agent has no MOC entry points for retrieval — falls back to grep

**Stepping stone:** yes — opens future possibilities

**Stepping-stone basis:** Frontmatter schema and lifecycle states are identical to what V-A, V-B, and V-C all require — fully forward-compatible with any future choice

## Dominated Variant Elimination

- PARA + Zettelkasten Split (IPARAG) — Constraint violation: migration cost = high. Imposing PARA buckets on the existing Cyrillic role structure requires full restructure, violating the hard constraint. Eliminated before Pareto computation.
- Minimal Substrate + Deferred Methodology — dominated by Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid. Dominated by V-A on all target dimensions. The 'emerge from use' premise is the documented path to permanent filing-cabinet status.

## Pareto Front Trade-Offs

- Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid — Pays med curation burden (weekly 30-min review, MOC creation discipline) to get high thinking-partner yield, high 5-year survival, and high agent retrieval fit. The right choice if Job 1 (thinking partner) is genuinely the primary goal.
- Karpathy LLM-Wiki Pattern — Pays low curation burden, preserves agent retrieval fit, but sacrifices thinking-partner yield and 5-year survival track record. Right choice only if the user wants a queryable archive of raw fragments, not personal authorship of ideas.
- PARA + Zettelkasten Split (IPARAG) — Eliminated by constraint (migration cost high). Would otherwise score high on ops-hub fit and thinking-partner fit but fails on 5-year survival and violates the no-full-restructure constraint.
- Minimal Substrate + Deferred Methodology — Dominated by V-A on all targets. Legitimate only as a temporary stepping stone if commitment to V-A feels premature — but its 'minimal substrate' is identical to what V-A starts with anyway.

**Incomparable pairs:**
- Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid vs Karpathy LLM-Wiki Pattern

**Selection policy:** Maximize thinking-partner yield first; maximize agent retrieval fit second; minimize curation burden third. Hard constraint: migration cost must be low or medium (no full restructure of existing vault).

**Recommendation (advisory):** Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid

**Recommendation rationale:** V-A wins on the primary target (thinking-partner fit: high vs low for V-B), ties on agent retrieval fit (both high), and wins on 5-year survival (high vs med). The only cost is med curation burden — real but within the stated 45-min/week budget. V-B's sole advantage is lower maintenance, at the cost of converting Job 1 from thinking-partner to queryable archive of raw fragments, which contradicts the primary goal.
