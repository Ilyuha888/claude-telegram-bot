# Solutions for: Choose a PKM methodology to give the vault operational semantics

Problem: prob-20260417-001

## Variants (4)

### V-A. Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid

Statement-titled atomic evergreen notes linked by wikilinks. MOCs as navigational hubs (structured entry points for the agent). GTD inbox-processing for Telegram captures. Four-state lifecycle: inbox→draft→evergreen→archived. Operations handled as typed notes (type: project/person) with task checklists inside project notes. Agent maintains topic-index.md in Agent vault, proposes links and MOC updates, flags stale inbox items weekly. Weekly ~30 min curation review.

**Strengths:**
- Highest thinking-partner yield in literature
- MOCs are best-known structure for LLM agent navigation
- Statement titles improve keyword retrieval
- 4-state lifecycle maps directly to capture→refine pipeline
- Stepping stone to embeddings or LLM-wiki later

**Novelty:** Unifies all four jobs under one framework; MOCs serve as both human navigation and the agent's structured entry point

**Weakest link:** MOC maintenance discipline — if MOCs fall stale, agent retrieval degrades silently and the navigational advantage collapses

**Risks:**
- MOC creation discipline required — easy to defer
- Committing cold: if MOC discipline doesn't fit your rhythm after 3 months, you've wasted 3 months

**Stepping stone:** yes — opens future possibilities

**Stepping-stone basis:** Frontmatter schema and lifecycle states are fully forward-compatible with adding GraphRAG, embeddings, or LLM-wiki compilation later

### V-B. Karpathy LLM-Wiki Pattern

Human owns raw/ folder only (captures, voice memos, fragments). Agent compiles and maintains a cross-linked wiki in Agent_Obsidian_Vault/wiki/. Agent detects contradictions, creates cross-references, maintains the compiled knowledge base. Human rarely edits compiled pages. Capture pipeline: Telegram → raw/ → agent compile → wiki page with 8-15 wikilinks per page.

**Strengths:**
- Lowest human authoring burden
- Agent compilation produces dense cross-referencing automatically
- raw/ pattern is durable regardless of future methodology changes

**Novelty:** Inverts the authorship model — agent is primary author of structured knowledge; human authors only raw inputs

**Weakest link:** Semantic drift — iterative LLM rewriting smooths nuance and homogenizes voice (Nature 2024); defeats generation-effect cognitive benefit

**Risks:**
- LLM semantic drift over time
- Human loses direct authorship of their own thinking
- No 5-year practitioner track record

### V-C. PARA + Zettelkasten Split (IPARAG)

Explicit dual-mode structure: PARA (Projects/Areas/Resources/Archives) for operational notes (Job 2) + separate Zettelkasten 'Galaxy' zone for idea development (Job 1). Different conventions per zone: operational notes are date-prefixed and action-oriented; Zettelkasten notes are atomic, statement-titled. Requires restructuring the existing Cyrillic role layout into PARA buckets — the user is open to this. Agent supports both zones with different navigation strategies.

**Strengths:**
- Strongest operational structure for Job 2 (projects, areas, tasks)
- Classic Zettelkasten for Job 1 is proven
- Clear zone boundaries reduce agent write-permission ambiguity

**Novelty:** Separates operational and conceptual knowledge into structurally distinct zones rather than unifying under one methodology — explicitly designed for the tension between Job 1 and Job 2

**Weakest link:** PARA boundary ambiguity — the Resource vs. Area vs. Project distinction causes documented 3-year collapse from reorganization churn, regardless of initial structure quality

**Risks:**
- PARA's documented 3-year collapse rate from reorganization churn
- Two mental models increases cognitive overhead and methodology-hopping risk
- High upfront migration cost from current Cyrillic role structure

### V-D. Staged: Minimal Substrate → V-A

Phase 1 (3 months): add only frontmatter schema (type, status, created, source), 4-state lifecycle, and 15 seed tags. Agent files captures into 00-inbox/, flags stale items, proposes links opportunistically. No MOCs, no statement titles yet. Observe actual capture patterns, retrieval needs, and curation rhythm. Phase 2: transition to V-A (Evergreen-MOC-GTD) with evidence about your actual usage rather than theory. The key insight: V-D's minimal substrate is identical to V-A's first step — the commitment question is whether to impose the full discipline cold or let 3 months of evidence guide the rollout of MOCs and statement-title conventions.

**Strengths:**
- Lowest upfront commitment and migration cost
- Hedges against the perfectionist's fallacy and methodology-hopping
- Identical substrate to V-A — zero migration cost if you do transition
- 3 months of evidence makes the V-A commitment much more durable

**Novelty:** Reframes the methodology choice as a probe strategy — delays full commitment until after observed behavior, reducing methodology-hopping risk at the cost of delayed thinking-partner yield

**Weakest link:** Emergence deferral — 'observe for 3 months then decide' often becomes 'never decided'; without an explicit transition trigger the vault stays in filing-cabinet mode indefinitely

**Risks:**
- Thinking-partner yield is deferred 3+ months
- Agent retrieval stays at grep-level during Phase 1
- Transition trigger must be explicit or Phase 2 never happens

**Stepping stone:** yes — opens future possibilities

**Stepping-stone basis:** Phase 1 substrate is identical to V-A's starting point — transitioning to V-A costs zero rework

## Dominated Variant Elimination

- PARA + Zettelkasten Split (IPARAG) — dominated by Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid. V-C's sole strength over V-A is ops-hub fit (high vs med). But V-A beats V-C on 5 of 6 remaining dimensions: retrieval (high vs med), curation burden (med vs high), 5-year survival (high vs low), migration cost (med vs high), hopping risk (med vs high). PARA's documented 3-year collapse is structural — it happens even when the initial structure is clean — which makes V-C's ops-hub advantage a short-term win that costs long-term coherence. Eliminated from the operative front, though its ops-hub insight survives: V-A can be augmented with explicit project/area note conventions to close the gap.

## Pareto Front Trade-Offs

- Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid — Best thinking-partner yield + agent retrieval fit + 5-year survival. Pays med curation burden, med migration cost, and med hopping risk (committing cold). Right choice if you trust your discipline and want compounding thinking-yield to start immediately.
- Karpathy LLM-Wiki Pattern — Best curation burden, migration cost, hopping risk. Pays thinking-partner yield (low — human authors only raw fragments) and ops-hub fit (low). Right choice only if the vault is primarily a queryable archive, not a place where you develop original thinking.
- Staged: Minimal Substrate → V-A — Staged probe to V-A. Wins on curation burden (low), migration cost (low), hopping risk (low — evidence-based commitment is more durable). Pays deferred thinking-partner yield (med in phase 1, high after transition to V-A) and deferred agent retrieval fit. Right choice if you're genuinely uncertain whether MOC discipline will stick, and you want observed behavior to anchor the V-A commitment.

**Selection policy:** Maximize thinking-partner yield first; maximize agent retrieval fit second; minimize curation burden third. Migration cost is a regular target. Methodology-hopping risk is an observation dimension.

**Recommendation (advisory):** Evergreen-MOC-GTD Hybrid

**Recommendation rationale:** Under the stated policy, V-A wins the primary target (thinking-partner fit: high) and ties V-D on it (med, not high in phase 1). V-D is the legitimate alternative if methodology-hopping risk is the primary concern — the 3-month probe costs zero rework since its substrate is identical to V-A's starting point. Both V-A and V-D are credible picks; the choice is a confidence question, not a methodology question.
